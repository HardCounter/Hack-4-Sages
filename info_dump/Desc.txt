While thousands of exoplanets have been discovered, assessing their potential to support life is severely bottlenecked by traditional 3D climate models, like NASA's ROCKE-3D, which require days of supercomputing per planet. To solve this, our team want to build an Autonomous Exoplanetary Digital Twin near real-time, browser-based platform that will simulate alien climates instantly.
At the core of our system will sit a domain-specialized LLM agent, such as AstroLLaMA, operating in an autonomous reasoning loop. Researchers will be able to explore planets using plain language, while the agent autonomously queries the NASA Exoplanet Archive and computes habitability indices without hardcoded scripts. To ensure scientific rigor, strict programmatic validators will act as guardrails, guaranteeing the LLM cannot hallucinate results that violate thermodynamic laws.
We will replace sluggish climate equations with an ensemble of Extreme Learning Machines (ELMs). This gradient-free approach will predict surface temperature distributions in milliseconds, capturing exotic tidally locked phases like "eyeball" or "lobster" ocean states. Furthermore, to address the severe statistical scarcity of habitable worlds in current catalogs, a generative adversarial network (CTGAN) will synthesize physically grounded planetary configurations to balance the data. Ultimately, the thermal data will be mapped onto a fully interactive 3D globe with scientifically calibrated colormaps.
We have designed this architecture specifically for a 72-hour sprint. The system will be strictly modular with built-in graceful degradation. If we struggle with completing our most ambitious initial assumptions, we have alternative paths and simplified solutions ready to deploy, guaranteeing a scientifically rigorous, working product at every single development stage.
