## Vocab and Keywords

### 1. Interior-Surface-Atmosphere (ISA) Interactions

* **Who it targets:** Marie-Luise Steinmeyer (Lead Astrophysicist)
* **Why it works:** Steinmeyer’s actual research heavily focuses on how a planet's rocky interior and surface chemistry affect its atmosphere (especially on super-Earths and sub-Neptunes). If your digital twin only simulates the *sky* (atmosphere) without accounting for the *ground* (geochemistry/volcanism), she will view it as too simplistic.
* **How to use it in your script:** > *"Unlike basic climate models, our digital twin accounts for complex **Interior-Surface-Atmosphere interactions**, allowing us to simulate how geological activity on a sub-Neptune might replenish potential biosignature gases over time."*

### 2. Photochemical False-Positive Mitigation

* **Who it targets:** Janusz Petkowski (Lead Astrobiologist)
* **Why it works:** Petkowski is acutely aware of the "false positive" problem in astrobiology (e.g., when a dead planet produces a gas that *looks* like a biological signature due to pure chemistry or starlight). Using this phrase shows him that your digital twin isn't just blindly looking for "life"—it is rigorously testing the chemistry to rule out non-biological sources.
* **How to use it in your script:** > *"To ensure data robustness, our model includes a **photochemical false-positive mitigation** layer, cross-referencing host-star radiation with the planet's atmospheric spectra to rule out abiotic gas production."*

### 3. Real-Time Data Assimilation (or Multiscale Modeling)

* **Who it targets:** Jian Gong (Digital Twin & System Modeling Expert)
* **Why it works:** In the scientific community, a "Digital Twin" is not just a 3D visualization; it is a dynamic system that constantly updates itself as new data comes in. Gong will be looking for proof that your model can ingest new data (like from the James Webb Space Telescope) and update its predictions dynamically.
* **How to use it in your script:** > *"The core architecture relies on **real-time data assimilation**. As new spectroscopic data is published from missions like JWST, the digital twin automatically updates its habitability parameters without requiring a manual system rebuild."*

### 4. Anomaly Detection in Imbalanced Datasets

* **Who it targets:** Ravi Changle & Dariusz Plewczynski (AI / ML Experts)
* **Why it works:** The biggest problem in predicting exoplanet habitability using AI is that 99.9% of planets are dead. This creates an "imbalanced dataset," which breaks standard machine learning models. Mentioning how you handled this proves to the AI experts that your methodology is mature and production-ready.
* **How to use it in your script:** > *"Because habitable exoplanets are statistically rare, we utilized advanced **anomaly detection to handle highly imbalanced datasets**, preventing our neural network from overfitting to barren, hot-Jupiter profiles."*
