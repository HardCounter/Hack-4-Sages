# Skeleton

## Steinmeyer

- ISA (Interior Surface Atmosphere)
- Sub Neptunes
- Super Earths
- Radius Gap phenomenon (Fulton Gap)
- sulfur chemistry constraints
- Carbon to Oxygen C/O




The application must simulate sulfur chemistry as a primary constraint. In the model, surface minerals must be explicitly linked to atmospheric states. The digital twin should categorize these into three primary regimes for linkage: hydrogen-rich atmospheres, oxygen-rich atmospheres, and atmospheres characterized by the coexistence of methane ($CH_4$) and carbon dioxide ($CO_2$). 

Cinematically, the video must show the digital twin predicting the formation of sulfur cloud condensates. As user inputs or automated multi-agent systems alter the planet's equilibrium temperature ($T_{eq}$) and surface pressure within the user interface, the visualizer should dynamically render the condensation of hydrogen sulfide ($H_2S$) and sulfuric acid ($H_2SO_4$).
